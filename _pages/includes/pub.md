**SIGMOD 2026** <span class='badge'>SIGMOD 2026</span>

[**GraphTwin: Cache-Centric Bit-Level Graph Representation for Fast and Exact Graph Queries**](https://example.com) \\
*Sijin Wang* (second author), Can Lu, Yikai Zhang, et al.

- **Brief introduction**: Motivated by the “memory-wall” bottleneck of adjacency lists, we design GraphTwin – a hybrid graph representation combining cache-optimized k-bit vectors and memory-resident adjacency lists. This enables 95% of negative edge queries (dominant in sparse graphs) to be resolved in 1 CPU cycle via in-cache bitwise‑AND operations.
- **My contributions**: Optimized GraphTwin building algorithms; designed solutions for triangle counting and set inclusion; conducted all experiments; participated in paper writing.

**WWW 2026** <span class='badge'>WWW 2026</span>

[**Network Dismantling via Reverse Dismantling: Static and Dynamic Algorithms**](https://example.com) \\
*Sijin Wang* (second author), Can Lu, Yikai Zhang, et al.

- **Brief introduction**: We propose a novel “reverse‑dismantling” perspective that converts the NP‑hard Network Dismantling problem into an incremental insertion process. Our insertion‑based greedy algorithm and lightweight tree index achieve state‑of‑the‑art accuracy and 1–2 orders of magnitude speedup on graphs with up to 20 million edges.
- **My contributions**: Proposed efficient algorithms for the ND problem; proved polynomial delay time complexity; conducted all experiments.

[**Efficient Dense Diverse Subgraph Search in Attributed Graphs**](https://example.com) \\
*Sijin Wang* (second author), Can Lu, Yikai Zhang, et al.

- **Brief introduction**: This paper studies diversified subgraph search to avoid minority‑group bias. We propose the problem of searching for the densest at‑least‑k‑vertex subgraph and develop two approximation algorithms. Experiments demonstrate effectiveness and efficiency.
- **My contributions**: Assisted in implementation; proved NP‑hardness of the problem.
